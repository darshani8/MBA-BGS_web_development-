Inside the BGSCET B-Plan Challenge: Where MBA Ideas Become Startups

CampusMay 12, 2026·5 min read·By BGSCET Entrepreneurship & Incubation Cell

Every great venture starts as a half-formed idea scribbled in a notebook. Our annual B-Plan Challenge exists to take those ideas seriously — to give student founders the structure, mentorship and stage they need to turn a hunch into a fundable business. Here is what happens inside one of the most exciting events on the BGSCET MBA calendar.

From idea to pitch in eight weeks

The challenge runs over eight intense weeks. Teams of three to four students begin with nothing but a problem they care about. They validate it with real customers, size the market, build a business model and a financial plan, and refine a pitch — all while juggling their regular coursework. It is demanding by design, because that is what building anything real feels like.

Every venture begins with a problem worth solving. The B-Plan Challenge is where our students find theirs — and learn to defend it.

Mentorship that makes the difference

What sets the programme apart is access. Each team is paired with mentors drawn from our faculty, our alumni network and Bengaluru's startup ecosystem. These mentors do not hand out answers; they ask the hard questions investors will ask — Who exactly is your customer? Why now? What is your unfair advantage? Learning to handle that scrutiny early is half the value of the experience.

The showcase

The challenge culminates in a live showcase where finalist teams pitch to a panel of investors, entrepreneurs and industry leaders. Six minutes on stage, followed by a rapid-fire question round. The energy in the room is electric — and the feedback is honest. Winning teams earn incubation support, seed mentorship and, for the strongest ideas, introductions to early-stage investors.

What students actually take away

Not every team launches a company, and that is fine. The deeper goal is to build an entrepreneurial mindset that serves students everywhere — in startups and in large organisations alike. Participants walk away with skills that are hard to teach in a lecture:

  • Customer discovery — talking to real users before building anything.
  • Financial modelling — turning assumptions into a credible plan.
  • Storytelling — making people care about your idea in minutes.
  • Resilience — handling tough questions and bouncing back from a no.

Why it matters for your MBA

Recruiters and investors alike value people who can spot a problem, structure a solution and rally a team around it. The B-Plan Challenge compresses that entire journey into one semester — and it is open to every BGSCET MBA student, regardless of specialization. Whether you dream of founding a company or leading intrapreneurial projects inside a large firm, this is where you learn to turn ideas into outcomes.

Have an idea you have been sitting on? Bring it to campus. The next challenge could be where it finally takes shape.
